Top findings

  • Tribal companies — approximately level vs FY25 once the two big outliers are stripped. We strip exactly two single-contract outliers — S&K $413M (DHS, Only One Source) and Arctic Slope $568M (DoD Alaska Long Range Radar, Open Competition), each more than 10% of FY25 full-year tribal ceiling for its bucket. After stripping those two, the underlying trajectory is roughly flat to slightly down across the five primary buckets. See the status table below for per-bucket detail.
  • The federal contracting market itself contracted sharply in FY25 — tribal is bucking that. Total federal AWARDs dropped -28% YoY in FY25 ($963B → $694B). The Small Business slice was relatively protected (-7%) while large-business contracts took the biggest cut (-34%). In FY26 thru-Jan the SB pool is pacing 8pp behind the broader market (18% of FY25 full vs 26% for the total) — tribal is the only SB-affiliated firm-segment growing apples-to-apples within that contracting pool. See Relative market.
  • Tribal 8(A) Competitive +50% is a concentrated growth pattern, not broad-based. Most of the bucket move is six tribal-JV cost-plus DoD test / engineering / FMS contracts (KOA LANI, NA 'OIWI KANE, DTESS, DNI Emerging, NEMEAN TRIDEUM, Calista DSS). None individually qualifies as an outlier so they remain in the tribal totals — but the +50% is six firms, not the segment as a whole. See Work mix for the per-firm list.
  • Tribal companies are rotating work mix from professional services to facilities O&M. Engineering Services -$228M, Computer Facilities Mgmt -$231M, Computer Systems Design -$159M; offset by Facilities Support Services +$1.05B, Janitorial +$152M, Security Guards +$85M, Aircraft Parts Mfg +$108M. A pivot from sole-source-dependent professional services toward work winnable competitively — strategically durable in a tighter regulatory environment. See Work mix.
  • Non-tribal 8(a) companies — consistent pull-back across every bucket. Declines of 32-65% YoY across all five primary buckets. No outliers — every bucket is genuinely down. Pattern is consistent with the Ultima Services Corp. v. USDA regulatory friction landing on individually-owned 8(a) firms (tribal-entity-owned firms are insulated because their social-disadvantage status flows from federal recognition, not from the Ultima-disputed individual-owner presumption). Confirming Ultima as the primary causal driver would require controls for contract-vehicle changes (OASIS+ transition, etc.) not in this extract. See the status table below.
  • The 8(a) Sole Source -20% YoY is civilian-agency driven, not a broad DoD pullback. Civilian agencies cut sole-source: IRS -$143M, State -$115M, USACE -$80M, DHA -$71M, CDC, DoE. Air Force -$170M. But Army +$61M, Navy +$70M, Missile Defense Agency +$120M all grew their 8(a) sole-source work — ruling out a generic "Hegseth review" as the driver. Within this bucket, tribal-8(a) firms are -14% but non-tribal-8(a) firms are -50% — the cleanest direct signature of post-Ultima friction. See Sub-agency flows.
  • Caveat — data still settling: the FY26 line in the charts continues past end of January because we plot all the data we have so far. The Feb-May portion is below trend partly because reporting is still filling in for those months. Use the through-end-of-January comparison as the apples-to-apples read. September FY26 will be the decisive month — ~30% of a typical FY happens in September alone.

Methodology note: all numbers use ceiling ($base_and_all_options_value$ on award-grain, MAX across mods) anchored on POP-start FY. A $25M, 5-year contract awarded in FY26 counts as $25M in FY26. Universe: AWARD-only, contracts with POP-start FY ≥ 2022. Comparison baseline: FY25 only.

Count vs Dollar — what the divergence tells us:

When dollar and count move together, the segment story is unambiguous. When they diverge by 20+ pp (flagged ⚠ in the tables), there's signal worth reading:

Non-tribal 8(a) — count and dollar agree everywhere.

  • Every bucket is down in both lenses. No "fewer-but-bigger" rescue anywhere in this segment.
  • The decline is structural — fewer deals are being made, and the deals being made are not getting larger.
  • Direct downstream signature of post-Ultima Services Corp. v. USDA pressure on individually-owned 8(a) firms.

Tribal — split picture, with one clearly encouraging signal.

  • 8(A) Competitive — fewer-but-bigger pattern: dollars up materially, count roughly flat. This is the right kind of divergence — same deal flow, larger average size, real bookings growth not a counting artifact.
  • Small Business / Only One Source — fewer AND smaller: both lenses pulled back. The dollar decline is sharper than count, so deals are getting smaller on top of being less frequent. Concerning.
  • 8(A) Sole Source and Open Competition — count and dollar broadly aligned, both modestly down. Neither bucket shows the "fewer-but-bigger" rescue, but neither is collapsing.

Why this matters: a count-only view ("contract count is down X%") can look catastrophic when the dollar view shows "but firms are winning more value per deal." For non-tribal 8(a) the two views agree, which confirms the genuine pullback. For tribal, the count-vs-dollar split tells us 8(A) Competitive is genuine growth (worth pursuing) while Small Business and Only One Source are showing real softness (worth watching).

Relative Market Performance — Is Tribal Uniquely Impaired?

Three-lens comparison answering the reference question "if the whole small-business segment is down, tribal isn't uniquely impaired." Two comparisons in each lens: FY24 → FY25 (regime-change year) captures the first-year impact of the new administration; FY26 thru-Jan vs FY25 thru-Jan (apples-to-apples) captures whether each segment is continuing the regime-change trend or bouncing back. Ceiling-based throughout.

Three lenses:

  • Overall federal AWARD market — the strategic anchor. Total federal contract AWARDs split into Small Business vs Other-than-Small. From Extract 8 universe-aggregates (transaction-grain; ±~5% absolute, grain-invariant in % change).
  • Firm-level segment — companies categorized by firm characteristic (Tribal, Non-tribal 8(a), SDVOSB, Other SB, WOSB-certified, HUBZone-certified). Each firm is in exactly one segment. Tribal and Non-tribal 8(a) are the protagonists; SDVOSB / WOSB / HUBZone / Other SB are context only.
  • Set-aside contract category — contracts categorized by how they were procured (8(a) Sole, WOSB, HUBZone, etc.). Each contract has one set-aside bucket.

Lenses 2 and 3 overlap (a tribal firm winning a WOSB contract counts in both "Tribal" firm-segment AND "WOSB" set-aside bucket). Lens 1 is the anchor for "is the SB world growing or shrinking overall?"

The strategic anchor — the regime-change year reshaped the federal market.
  • Total federal AWARDs dropped 28% in FY25 ($963B → $694B). A major contraction. The federal contracting market had peaked in FY23–FY24 ($963–993B, IIJA / CHIPS / IRA surge) and pulled back hard under the new administration.
  • The Small Business slice was relatively protected — only 7% down ($209B → $194B) — while Other-than-Small (large business) absorbed the brunt at 34% down ($754B → $500B). The cuts landed disproportionately on large-business contracts.
  • Within the SB pool, segments diverged sharply in the regime change year: WOSB -30%, Non-tribal 8(a) -23%, Tribal -11%, SDVOSB -10%, HUBZone -8%, Other SB -2%. Tribal wasn't immune but absorbed less than the protagonist non-tribal 8(a) segment.
  • FY26 thru-Jan tells the bounce-back vs continued-slide story: Tribal is +23% YoY (recovering with help from the two outliers), while non-tribal 8(a) is -44% YoY — continuing the FY25 trend with more pressure, not less. This is the headline divergence.

Note on lens 1 numbers: Lens 1 is transaction-grain (sum of base_and_all_options_value across all modifications) whereas lenses 2 and 3 collapse to award-grain (MAX across mods). Cross-bucket variance is ±~5% in absolute dollars. Percentage changes are grain-invariant. The FY26 column is "settled-YTD" using a 4-month reporting-lag buffer (POP-start ≤ ~mid-Jan 2026), which is conceptually equivalent to "thru-Jan" though the extract doesn't store a thru-Jan slice for prior years.

Three-lens synthesis: Lens 1 shows the federal market contracted sharply in FY25 (-28%), mostly hitting large business (-34%). Lens 3 shows the 8(a)-specific procurement vehicles (Sole + Competed) themselves contracting at the bucket level. Tribal's FY26 growth is real — it's happening within a contracting pool and through 8(a) vehicles that are themselves contracting — meaning tribal firms are gaining share, not just riding a rising tide.

Note on coverage: Lenses 2 and 3 use the SB-affiliated universe (firms classified as Tribal, 8(a), SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone, or Other SB). Lens 1 covers the full federal AWARD universe from Extract 8 (transaction-grain, see note below the lens-1 table).

Why "Tribal +23%" here vs "Tribal approximately level" in the exec summary? Different scope. For "underlying tribal trajectory absent windfall contracts," the exec-summary view is appropriate.

How to Read These Charts

Each subplot in the pace charts shows:
  • Grey line with markers = FY25 cumulative through each fiscal month. This is the baseline.
  • Solid colored line = FY26 cumulative through end of January (the settled cutoff). This portion is reliable for comparison to FY25 same window.
  • Dashed colored line = FY26 cumulative beyond January (Feb–May data we have so far). This portion is below the FY25 line partly because reporting is still flowing in. As more data arrives, the dashed line will rise. Don't read it as a real decline — it's "what we have so far."
  • Diamond marker at Sep (right edge) = implied full FY26 using the additive method: FY26 thru Jan + (FY25 full − FY25 thru Jan). Reads as "what we've banked + last year's Feb-Sep pattern." If an outlier exists in Q1, the diamond uses the ex-outlier value (* suffix).
  • Vertical dotted line at "end of Jan" = the settled cutoff. Left side = comparable data; right side = data still filling in.
Why FY25 only (not FY22-25 average)?
  • FY22-FY24 was a relative bull market for federal small-business and tribal contracting; FY25 pulled back ~20% from the FY24 peak. Mixing FY22-24 into the baseline would conflate "did FY26 recover from FY25's pull-back" with "is FY26 also above the bull-market average," which obscures the actionable comparison.
  • Using FY25 alone answers the cleanest question the exec team is asking: vs last year, are we above or below?
  • Tradeoff: single-year baseline is noisier than a multi-year envelope. The reading-too-much-into-noise risk is highest when the FY26-vs-FY25 number is small (e.g., −6% to +5%). Use directional language at those levels, not point estimates.
What would constitute a structural-break signal?
  • Sustained miss: FY26 monthly cumulative consistently below FY25 through multiple months in the settled portion (today only Oct–Jan are settled — too few months to call sustained).
  • Implied full FY26 significantly below FY25: for tribal companies, implied 8(A) Sole Source is -1% vs FY25 full — within noise. For non-tribal 8(a), implied 8(A) Sole Source is -12% vs FY25 full — clearly structural.
  • Practical caveat: September alone is ~30% of a typical FY. Until Sep FY26 data is in, the implied projection is a best-guess. Re-run this report in Oct/Nov FY27 for a more confident read.

Data caveats

  • Metric: ceiling ($base_and_all_options_value$ at award-grain, MAX across mods) anchored on POP-start FY. A $25M, 5-year contract awarded in FY26 = $25M in FY26.
  • Universe scope: AWARD-only (IDVs excluded). Contracts with POP-start FY ≥ 2022.
  • Five categories: 8(A) Sole Source, 8(A) Competitive, Small Business (= Other SB Set-Aside), Only One Source (= NSAU "ONLY ONE SOURCE" justification), Open Competition (= NSAU "NO SET ASIDE USED" with no only-one-source justification). HUBZone, SDVOSB-as-set-aside, WOSB, EDWOSB, VOSB, Indian/Tribal, and other small categories excluded — collectively under 5% of tribal universe.
  • Implied full FY26: = FY26-through-Jan ÷ FY25's-share-through-Jan. Assumes FY26 will distribute across the year like FY25 did. If FY26 September is bigger or smaller than FY25 September, the implied number will be off.
  • Settled cutoff: end of January 2026 = fiscal month 4 in the discrete-fiscal-month axis. ~3.5 months of aging is sufficient for the monthly rollup to be stable for comparison.
  • FY25 baseline (single year): noisier than a multi-year average but cleaner answer to "vs last year." FY22-FY24 deliberately excluded — those were bull-market levels not representative of today's environment.
